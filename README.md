1. Overview

This project focuses on analyzing a comprehensive global restaurant dataset containing information on over 9,500 restaurants across multiple countries and cities.
The goal of this analysis is to understand restaurant trends, customer preferences, pricing behavior, and factors that influence customer satisfaction through ratings.
Insights from this study can help restaurant owners and food delivery platforms improve their service offerings and customer experiences.

2. Data Source

The dataset was obtained from an open-source restaurant data repository.
It includes information about restaurant names, locations, cuisines, average costs, ratings, and service options.

Key details:

Total Records: 9,551

Total Features: 21

File Format: CSV

Columns include:

Restaurant ID, Name, Country, City, Locality,

Average Cost for Two, Currency,

Has Table Booking, Has Online Delivery,

Aggregate Rating, Rating Text, Rating Color, Votes, etc.

This dataset enables meaningful cross-country and cross-city comparisons to analyze restaurant patterns and customer behavior.

3. Project Overview

The project aims to extract insights from restaurant data to identify patterns and relationships among various factors such as cuisine type, pricing, and ratings.

Objectives:

Understand restaurant distribution across cities and countries.

Identify the most popular and top-rated cuisines.

Analyze how pricing and delivery options impact customer ratings.

Examine the relationship between cost, votes, and overall restaurant performance.

Tools Used:

Python: Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly

SQL: For analytical queries and aggregations

Power BI / Tableau: For dashboard and visualization

5. Key Findings

Indian and Italian cuisines were among the most popular across multiple cities.

Restaurants offering online delivery received more customer votes, indicating higher engagement.

The average cost for two people was not directly proportional to better ratings ‚Äî even low-cost restaurants achieved high satisfaction scores.

Table booking availability correlated with slightly higher ratings, especially in premium price ranges.

Cities with the highest average ratings had a strong presence of diverse cuisines and delivery options.
